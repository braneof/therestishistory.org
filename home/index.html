Polio

Polio epidemics plagued the United States during the first half of the 20th Century. The fear of polio swept the country and was a major worry of everyday life. Now a mostly forgotten disease, survivors tell about their sickness and their damaged bodies, and, having survived the ordeal, their eventual recovery with the expectation that the worst was behind them. But now, decades later, to their utter despair, they are discovering the ravages of post-polio syndrome.

The Break

What makes the difference between failure and success? Luck? Genes? An inheritance? Those all may help, but at some point, every successful person, somehow and in some way, gets a break. That break can end up making all the difference. Stories in “The Break” examines people who were successful because someone gave them a break.
